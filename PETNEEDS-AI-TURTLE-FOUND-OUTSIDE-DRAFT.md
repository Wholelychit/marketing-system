# Turtle Found Outside: What To Do Next

Finding a turtle outside can make you want to help right away.

Sometimes the best help is to leave it alone.

Sometimes the turtle may need safe help from a wildlife rescue, veterinarian, or local animal authority.

> **Pet safety note:** This page gives general animal-care information. It does not replace a veterinarian, wildlife rehabilitator, or local animal authority.

## Quick Answer

If the turtle is healthy and not in danger, leave it where you found it.

If it is crossing a road, help it cross in the same direction it was already going, only if you can do so safely.

If the turtle is injured, bleeding, cracked, weak, or in a dangerous place, contact a wildlife rehabilitator, veterinarian, animal control, or local rescue resource.

## Do Not Take It Home As A Pet

A turtle found outside may be a wild animal.

Taking it home can harm the turtle and may be illegal in some places.

Wild turtles often need specific food, heat, water, habitat, and medical care.

## If The Turtle Is Crossing A Road

Only help if it is safe for you.

Steps:

1. Watch traffic first.
2. Pick up the turtle gently if needed.
3. Move it in the same direction it was already going.
4. Place it off the road.
5. Do not turn it around.
6. Do not move it far away.

A turtle may be heading to a nesting place, pond, or home range.

## If The Turtle Is Injured

Call for help if you see:

- cracked shell
- bleeding
- trouble moving
- swollen eyes
- attack injury
- fishing hook or line
- weakness
- trouble breathing
- severe distress

Do not try to glue, tape, or repair the shell yourself.

## If Children Found The Turtle

Teach children to look, not grab.

Turtles can be stressed by handling. Some may bite or scratch if scared.

Have children wash hands after being near wildlife.

## What Not To Do

Do not:

- take a wild turtle home as a pet
- paint the shell
- drill the shell
- feed random human food
- put a land turtle into deep water
- put an aquatic turtle far from water
- move it miles away
- handle it more than needed
- try home medical treatment

## Is It A Pet Turtle?

Sometimes a turtle outside may be an escaped pet.

Signs may include:

- very bright non-native markings
- found near homes or apartments
- unusually calm around people
- obvious tank-pet condition

Even then, contact a local rescue, vet, shelter, or animal control for the safest next step.

## What If It Is In My Yard?

If the turtle is not injured and not trapped, it may simply be passing through.

Keep pets and children away. Let the turtle move on.

## Common Questions

### Should I put the turtle in water?

Not always. Some turtles live mostly on land. Putting the wrong turtle into deep water can be dangerous.

### Can I keep it?

Do not assume you can keep it. A turtle found outside may be wild, protected, or difficult to care for properly.

### What should I feed it?

Do not feed random foods. The safest step is to contact a wildlife or reptile professional if the turtle appears to need help.

### What if my dog picked it up?

Check the turtle for injury and contact a veterinarian, wildlife rehabilitator, or rescue if there is any damage or distress.

## Final Reminder

If the turtle is healthy and safe, let it stay wild.

If it is injured or in danger, get qualified local help.
